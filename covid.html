Remote Tutoring and COVID-19

Here at eTutors we are aware of the challenges families may face with virtual learning. The lack of peer support being a major concern as students begin learning from home rather than the classroom. We want to try to focus on the positives during this time of transition but these are some changes we expect to see in education:

  • Self paced learning will be effective for multiple learning styles, which is a good thing. Students can work at their own pace and not feel pressured to keep up with the classroom setting. With this change there is a risk of widening achievement gaps, for example a student struggling with Algebra who has not caught up may feel lost when they start Geometry and other math courses. Don't struggle alone! Students will have to plan and communicate to stay ahead of learning.
  • We feel that the pass/fail system, as well as standardized tests will become less common (which we support). There will be more accountability for the individual's learning. College admissions may shift less from test scores and look at the student's hard work (and GPA) rather than their standardized test scores.
  • This time spent learning virtually will help students gain valuable technology skills, which will benefit them in future careers. Though we understand some districts do not provide devices for students which is a big problem with remote learning. Check out our resources page to learn about organizations helping with providing internet and/or computers.
